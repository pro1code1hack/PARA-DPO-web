WHAT IS PERSONAL DATA?

Personal and Special Category data is information about a person, generally defined as:
Forenames, surnames, DOBs, religion, locations, IP addresses, economic information
Ethnic background, political and philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, health, sex life, sexual orientation, gender

GDPR INCLUDES “PERSONALLY IDENTIFYABLE INFORMATION” OR PII
This has a huge impact across Housing Associations with data such as STAR surveys, e-mails discussing challenging tenants, and CCTV footage etc all in scope of these regulations.

WHY IS IT IMPORTANT TO HAVE A DPO?

A DPO is your organisation’s conduit to the ICO and a specialist in Data Protection.
If you process large scale special category and criminal data (e.g. health, racial or ethnic origin etc) then you need to have a DPO. ParaDPO believe all Housing Providers are large scale processors for this definition.

DPO’s provide expertise to manage Data Subject Rights, assess the risk and respond to personal data breaches, and conduct audits of Data Protection compliance.

A DPO will have everything prepared and ready in the event the ICO makes contact, for example records of processing or risk assessments for personal data breaches, or advice and Data Subject Rights logs.

Customers are becoming more aware of their rights and the demand will continue to grow.

A DPO can ensure that you appropriately discharge your rights and reduce the risk of having customer’s directing your organisation to the ICO.
